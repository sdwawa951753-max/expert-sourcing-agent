# Responsible Use

This tool is for **legitimate, personalized, one-to-one professional outreach** —
the kind a recruiter, chief of staff, or research-network lead does every day
when inviting a scholar to real, paid, opt-in work. Please keep it that way.

## What this tool does

- Uses **open, public** research data (OpenAlex, the Nobel Prize API).
- Surfaces contact channels the person has **themselves made public** for
  professional contact (ORCID, faculty pages, personal academic sites).
- **Drafts** messages for a **human to review and send**.

## What this tool deliberately does NOT do

- ❌ Guess or permute private email addresses at scale.
- ❌ Scrape data behind logins, paywalls, or against a site's Terms of Service.
- ❌ Collect personal (non-professional) contact details or home info.
- ❌ Send anything automatically, or send in bulk.

## Your responsibilities when you use it

- **Send few, send well.** This is not a spam cannon. The scoring exists to help
  you contact *fewer, better-matched* people.
- **Honor opt-outs immediately** and keep a suppression list.
- **Comply with the law** where you and the recipient are: e.g. **CAN-SPAM**
  (US), **GDPR / ePrivacy** (EU/UK) — which generally require a legitimate basis,
  clear identity, and an easy way to opt out for commercial contact.
- **Respect API terms & rate limits** (OpenAlex, Nobel, your search provider).
- **Be truthful.** Never fabricate credentials, mutual connections, or the offer.

## A simple test

If you wouldn't be comfortable with the recipient reading exactly how you found
them and why you're writing, don't send it. Good sourcing is something you can
explain proudly — not something you'd have to hide.
